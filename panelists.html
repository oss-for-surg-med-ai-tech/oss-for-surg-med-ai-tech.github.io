Panel Discussion

Moderator:
Ahmed Al-Hindawi
University College London
Panelists:
  • Jennifer Jiang-Kell – Healthchain
  • Thomas Wood – Fast Data Science
  • Philip Cosgriff – Consultant clinical scientist
  • Zachary Baum – Odin Vision (now part of Olympus Corporation)
  • Rachael Rodell – University College London
  • Shashank Sharma – CEO of Roboss Pte Ltd in Singapore

The panel discussion will last 45 minutes, starting with a one-minute introduction from each participant, with or without a short presentation. This will be followed by 30 minutes of discussion and five minutes for final thoughts and follow-up. The panel chair will introduce the questions below and welcomes contributions on any topics relevant to the workshop. We will document the challenges and key points raised in a work-in-progress white paper and invite both panellists and audience members to contribute to this vibrant discussion.

1. The Role of Open-Source in Advancing Surgical and Biomedical Technologies: The Current Status

How open-source software is transforming surgical robotics, medical imaging, and digital health.

Notable success stories (e.g., 3D Slicer, MONAI, OpenCV in medical imaging).

Overcoming barriers to adoption in hospitals and industry.

2. Future Trends and the Sustainability of Open-Source in Biomedical and Surgical AI

Emerging technologies shaping the next decade (e.g., federated learning, edge AI in surgery).

How to fund and maintain long-term open-source projects.

Predictions for industry adoption and potential disruptions.

3. AI, Open-Source, and Regulatory Challenges in Healthcare

How can open-source software and open-source AI models be used in regulated healthcare environments?

Case studies of AI frameworks in clinical trials and regulatory approval hurdles.

The tedious journey from an academic output to an approved clinical tool! Examples of success stories?

4. Other Topics

Tips for better open-source software (training, checklist, templates, etc).

Trends and future challenges: Using new software and hardware technologies; industry partnerships.

Geopolitical innovation strategies on open-source software, models, datasets, etc.
